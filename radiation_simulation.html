About this Simulation

This simulation demonstrates thermal radiation - the transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves (photons). All objects emit thermal radiation based on their temperature, following the Stefan-Boltzmann law. The simulation shows a hot object emitting photons in all directions, with an absorbing object that heats up as it absorbs radiation and cools down through passive cooling.

Observe how changing the temperature affects both the number of photons emitted and their energy (indicated by color). Notice how the distance between objects affects the amount of energy absorbed - radiation follows an inverse square law where intensity decreases with the square of the distance. Watch as the absorbing object's temperature rises when it absorbs photons and gradually cools down.

Use the icon to toggle the controls panel, and the icon to enter or exit fullscreen mode. The reset button () will restart the simulation with current settings.

Controls:

  • Speed: Adjusts the simulation rate. Higher values make time pass faster.
  • Temperature: Controls the temperature of the radiating object (now defaults to 3000K). Higher temperatures emit more photons with higher energy.
  • Photon Intensity: Adjusts the visual density of photons displayed (affects performance but not physics).
  • Object Distance: Controls how far the absorbing object is from the radiation source.
  • Cooling Rate: Controls how quickly the absorbing object loses heat to the environment (0-1000 Watts).
  • Absorption Object: Select different materials with varying absorption properties and thermal characteristics.

The simulation demonstrates key principles of thermal radiation including blackbody radiation, the Stefan-Boltzmann law (total power ∝ T⁴), the inverse square law for radiation intensity, and thermal equilibrium dynamics.
