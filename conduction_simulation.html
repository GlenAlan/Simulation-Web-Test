About this Simulation

This simulation models heat conduction through a 2D lattice of particles. Each particle is connected to its neighbors by springs. When you "heat" an area with your mouse (or touch), you impart kinetic energy to the particles in that region, causing them to vibrate more vigorously. This vibrational energy then propagates through the lattice via the spring connections, demonstrating how heat conducts through a solid material.

Controls:

  • Speed: Adjusts the simulation rate. Higher values make time pass faster.
  • Heating Radius: Controls the size of the area affected when you apply heat.
  • Heat Intensity: Determines how much energy is added to the particles when heating.
  • Avg. Temperature: Displays the average kinetic energy of all particles in the system, representing the overall "temperature" of the lattice.

Click and drag on the canvas to apply heat. Observe how the color of the particles changes based on their individual kinetic energy (amplitude of vibration), with hotter particles appearing redder and cooler ones bluer.
